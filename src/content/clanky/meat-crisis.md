Meat is one of the biggest problems humanity is facing these days. Every schnitzel, thigh, fish or any other type of meat we eat is responsible for death of a living animal. In order to produce enough meat for acceptable price, livestock are squeezed side by side so they can barely turn around. Animals are stressed out and they hurt each other. If anything we do today will be considered disgusting and immoral, it's probably our behaviour towards animals.
## Global impact of meat industry

Among the animals squeezed into a tiny space, diseases spread easily, and in order to survive at all it's necessary to pump them a huge amount of **antibiotics**, so huge that most of those are used for livestock farming. The problem is that when antibiotics are used in such amount, microbes become resilient to them and They might transmit diseases and cause epidemic or even pandemic, impossible to stop with antibiotics. 

Last but not least, meat contributes significantly to **climate change** – circa 15% of man-made greenhouse gas is caused by meat production. Similar percentage is produced by planes, cars and all the other modes of transportation together!

## Is vegetarianism the solution?

It must be clear to anyone who knows anything about meat consumption that it is no longer sustainable. Transition to vegetarianism or veganism may be the solution, but it's clear that majority of people is unwilling to do it - there are only few percent of those in Czechia and that is not enough to avert the crisis. And despite there are more and more people becoming vegetarians every year, meat consumption grows and it's clear that it will continue to grow even faster. 

## Cultured meat: hope for the future

Our case might look hopelessly. With growing world population it's necessary to produce increasingly more meat that people are unwilling to give up. However, one solution is within reach - **cultured or cultivated meat**.

The core problem of eating meat lies in the fact that it's extremely ineffective way of gaining necessary energy for our bodies. To get one kilogram of beef from a cow, we need to feed it with 25 kg of grains and 15000 litres of water! The reason is that cow consumes energy, that can otherwise use for meat growth, for many other "useless" things - it walks, thinks, breathes… But if we could avoid this step (cow) from the meat production, the crisis would be solved. 

And that is cultured meat in a nutshell - meat made with no cow, chicken, pig, or any other animal. Maybe it sounds like something impossible to you but do you know what is the best part about cultured meat? It already exists! And for a pretty long time - as early as 2013, one of the first cultured meat hamburgers was introduced to the world. Although back then, cultured meat wasn't designed for ordinary people, for its production cost more than 300 000 USD. However, the situation has been changing rapidly. In december 2020, sale of cultured chicken was authorized in Singapur as the first state in the world, with other states to follow it soon. Cultured meat is also cheaper - the _Eat Just_ company that produces it, can make one chicken nugget for 50 USD, which is not the cheapest nugget on the market, but it't much better that 300 000 USD before. 


## How is the cultured meat produced?

First, there are several cells extracted from the animal, whose meat we want to produce (few cells are enough, the animal hardly notices). Those are placed into a right environment that contains chemicals necessary for cell division and creation of the requested meat, which is exactly what cells do. In short, **cultured meat is cultivated**. The main obstacles that are facing cultured meat research, are securing its reasonable price and indistinguishable taste from live-stock meat. These issues have been resolving well though.
