Energy - a way to use forces other that our on physical. Usually, we chose a way that damages our planet. How do we damage it? How to create energy gently, economically and efectivelly? That, and much more, you will find out below. 

Since history, humans create energy. Back then it was fire for light and warm, whereas They had been using wood so that grew back in time without any trouble. Later we began to create energy in such way that it became devastating for Earth and life on her. We often use **black or brown coal** for heat generation hence we destroy not only air, but the whole climate. With coal we also generate share of elecricity. 


We keep wasting energy and moreover we generate it enviromentally unfriendly in most cases.  There are ways though. In our country the most common renewable sources of energy come from **sun, water and wind**. It's time to prefer those sources and replace both fossil fuels (coal, petrol or natural gas) and nuclear fission. 


## Renewable sources of energy

### Solar energy

Solar energy has been used recently more and more, we use it primarily for generating elektricity. We use solar panels. They are composed with photovoltaic cells that generate elektricity from sunlight. All technologies have some disadvantage though. 
There are 3 such downsides:

1. Intervention to the landscape both by the panels (it's better to install them on roofs) and mining of raw materials that are necessary for their production.
2. Panel longevity, that is around twenty years nowadays. However, researchers came up with how to recyclate panels, and hence it's possible to reuse material from panels that served out to a new functional one. 
3. Raw materials need to be mined somewhere, our case scenario is silicon and lithium, that we need to deliver, which cost a lot of money and is energetically unfeasible. 

In the Ore Mountains we have lithium deposit that should be used in a while, thereby the use of solar energy would increase. Solar energy is also used for water heating with solar thermal collectors.

### Hydroelectric energy

Another form of renewable energy is hydroelectric power. People came up with it  some time ago, and it works on a simple principle. Water flows into the turbine that rotates and generates electricity. You can't just put the turbine into a stream, you need to have strong current. In order to achieve strong current, you need a **steep slope and very much water**. Both these aspects are achieved by water dam, where they can regulate themselves. 

But once again, this source has some downsides. For the dam to create, you need to flood a valley with water, and also the dam costs plenty of money and material. Moreover, people living in the flooded valley lose their homes. Another big trouble is river deceleration and its ecosystem change, which might spell disaster for many species. Otherwise, the hydroelectric plant is a highly effective and efficient source. For example, the water plant _Mingechevir in Azerbajian_ supplies 90% of the country and also 30% of Georgia.

### Wind energy and others

Other important renewable sources include wind energy that use wind flow that rotates propeller connected into electric generator. This source too has its downsides. One of them is intervention into landscape, because the 25 metres tall wind turbine intervenes deeply only with its presence. Also there is no guarantee of power - there is no elektricity without wind. Its advantages are relatively cheap construction costs. Its durability is also limited but when there's regular maintenance, it may last a long time. 

In addition, there are even other renewable sources like **biomass, biogas** or here not very used **geothermal energy** či **sea wave energy**.

## Energy from non-renewable sources 

Now we're getting to the non-renewable sources, in Czechia especially coal and nuclear. They are higly effective but have great downsides and harm our planet. 


### Coal

The least ecological friendly way of generating electricity is coal. It is highly effective but also very harmful to the air. Recently some of the harmful chemicals from smokestacks filter but still it's very rightful to replace this energy source with renewable ones (nowadays the Czech government discusses whether They quit mining coal in 2033 or 2038).


### Nuclear energy

Another non-renewable source of energy is nuclear energy. This one works on fission of radioactive particles that produce much heat while splitting. This heat warms water that changes into steam and runs turbine. It's a very efficient power plant that may have great power.

It may have troubles though, for the radioactive chemicals might get released like when Chernobyl or Fukushima happened. Furthermore, the radioactive material - in our case uranium - needs to be mined and delivered somewhere. That mining intervenes into our landscape. Another trouble is the radioactive material that we don't know yet how to recycle and thus it's been stored underground.

### Petrol and natural gas

Non-renewable energy sources also include petrol and natural gas. Here gas is used for heat production, it's more eco-friendly than coal and petrol but still non-renewable fossil fuel. 

Electricity comes to our power outlet via high-voltage wires with significant losses. Thus it's best to use the closest power sources, installed directly on our roofs if possible.
