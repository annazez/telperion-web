Forest is a huge organism consisting of many different animals, plants, fungi, lichen and more. This system is balanced and there are various symbiotic and parasitic relations. It's also very fragile and any intervention might cause catastrophic impact for the forest. It's a cycle of life where every species has its role, and when it succeeds, it makes room to another one.

## Rainforests and its importance

Rainforests are exclusive for their versatility and biodiversity. More than half the plant species and majority of animals live in rainforests. We damage them, cut them down and replace them with palm plantations. Both rainforest and hope for keeping the climate change on leach are shrinking.

Every being in the rainforest has its chance and advantage. However, animals fight for food and plants for sunlight. There is no unused place in rainforest. Typical case is an old fallen tree on whose trunk and nutrients there are many different plants growing.

## Photosynthesis and deforestation

Inside every plant there's **photosynthesis**, in process during which carbon dioxide is absorbed and life-giving oxygen released. If it weren't for these plants, oxygen level in air would be such low that animals (humans included) wouldn’t be able to exist.

We replace these plants (trees above all) for vast supermarkets and parking lots. In Amazon and Indonesia hectares of rainforest have been disappearing with monocultures of **oil palm, sugar canes, eucalyptus** etc. taking its place. 70% to 90% of animals live inside rainforests. Up to 50% of rainforest have been cut down during the last 50 years. Animals go extinct by the thousands.

## Agriculture and its impact

Rainforest is unique in its versatility and biodiversity. Then why is this destruction happening? Where's the problem? How to fix it?

Eucalyptus is the crude material for non-recycled paper and eucalyptus oil. Deforestation is tolerated in many places. Brazil government allows clearing the rainforest and begin to grow agricultural crops in its place. Farmers burn the forest - often times much larger area than they need. When farmers abandon the area, rainforest grows again, but it's never such biodiverse.

This action makes a significant contribution not only to climate change, but it also affects animals living in the rainforests. No one asked them whether they have another place to go and we keep ruining their homes cold-bloodedly. Everything for a small harvest that can be grown in another place and manner, or to line the pockets of the mining companies. We consider it unjust and we want to change it.
