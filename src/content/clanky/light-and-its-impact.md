In order for light to create, some kind of energy is needed, mostly electric. Every one uses light but its also possible to save on electricity. Light can actually be harmful to people and also other animals. Why? How to use light properly?

It's an energy almost everyone uses, we light up chandelier, car lamp or a small flashlight. We take it for granted. All we have to do is raise our hand and push a switch or turn a knob. But we need to realize it's not granted. About one billion people in the world don't have access to electricity at all.

Many steps are necessary for the light to turn on in your room. The lights are powered by electricity but that needs to be produced somewhere, and a renewable or non-renewable source in needed for that. As new lights are developed, the heat-to-light ratio continues to decrease. Today we have energy-efficient bulbs that produce much heat for little energy. 

Produced light is a rare thing and we need to save energy and use it efficiently.

## Light pollution


Every year light polution increases. Aside from night skies where there are no stars visible, it negatively affects living organisms. Every  being needs light and darkness - a punctual variable dividing sunrise and sunset when light is warmest. Based on this organisms have evolved a system of time that impacts their behaviour and everyday functions.

This system needs information about the cycle of light and darkness so the circadian clock of the organisms could be in harmony with the sun cycle. Sunrise and sunset are thus an indicator by which daily biorhythm of every living being is regulated. This clock also senses when ratio of day and night is changing and informs us about the four seasons. Partially it impacts humankind, although it primarily affects other animals and plants. For example, trees winterize and animals gather fur in order to switch to winter mode in the right time. 

Man-made light also creates an artificial barrier that no bat or owl can cross, it gathers great amount of insect that insectivorous birds miss during daytime. 

The more light pollution there is, the less the circadian clock are sure whether it's day or night. Then they send inaccurate and unreliable information. This signal helps us operate our immune system, metabolism and other functions so  that organism can stay active during daytime and rest during night. Body regeneration depends not only on sleep but also time of the day and light. Thereby light pollution (not only light from cities and towns) can cause troubles.


## Blue light from LED lamps

Nowadays a big trouble comes from gradual installation of LED light. Comparing to other sodium-vapor lamps its benefits are longevity, lower energy consumption, better regulation and it also has a significant share of **blue light spectrum**.

Blue light is a strong signal for living beings. They are naturally adapted to perceive it as dawn. Our bodies understand blue light as a day and they start activity and pauses regeneration and recovery process. The sodium-vapor lamps in cities are replaced with cheap LED lamps and the problem keeps getting worse. The surrounding nature and people's health is unknowingly being harmed. 


## Street lightning


Urban lightning is the largest source of light pollution (there are more than 1 million public lights in Czechia). It's obvious that if reduction of negative impacts of artificial lighting is to be efficient, streetlights should  play a key role in that. The matter of street lightning is broad but nevertheless there should be these principles respected: 

1. Light only where it's needed
2. Only when it's needed
3. And only as much as is needed.

Consideration of where and why the lightning should be installed is one important step. Unfortunately, we can also find streetlights in places where its purpose is rather dubious, and conversely we lack them where they would be more than suitable.

For standard streetlights mainly the road should be lit and its closest surroundings, and its intensity should  correspond to the type and traffic density. Moreover, the lightning should be evenly spreaded and shouldn't dazzle drivers and pedestrians. For that purpose the functional lights are suited for streets and pavements and they can aim the light where it's needed. 

It's also important to take the blue light into account, because that's the biggest problem for living beings at night, therefore warmer lights (under 2800 Kelvin) are better.

## The effect of light on our health at home

Light pollution is a particular problem for nature and the surrounding area but its impact on human health begin already in our flat, house or work. When there's a long-term impact by the wrong light at the wrong time, it might result in bad sleep or depression. 

**Shift work** has a very strong impact on the body. While the organism faces constant light, it's desynchronized in about a month. Problems are becoming apparent, primarily ones related to sleep. People can't memorize or remember stuff,  feel bad, their appetites change and so on. 

The issue is mainly blue light at night that deceives our bodies about daytime and confuse our circadian clock. Our ploblem seems to be computer and phone screen use before bedtime and during night, mainly because of their blue light. The solution could therefore be various filters, orange-tinted glasses, mobile apps, or - ideally - avoiding their use for at least 90 minutes before bedtime.

### How to light properly?

- **White (blue) light**:  Use only during daytime (at workplace or in lamps).
- **Warm light**: Use at night (ideal lights in apartments are light-dimming and below 2500 K).
- **Red (or no) light**: Use at night or before bedtime.

Light intensity is also important, it should decrease together with light temperature - our eyes need less red light that the blue one to see things. Try it yourself and after 3 weeks you'll see the difference in your sleep, getting up and energy levels.
