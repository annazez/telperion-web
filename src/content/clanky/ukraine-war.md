War in Ukraine is undeniably a terrible humanitarian tragedy. Another tragedy is taking place in the background, just as scary to say the least. It's the ecological tragedy...

The russian invasion entails so many threaths for the enviroment that we wouldn't name all in this article. We picked only some of them. 

## Pollution

Mere ruins and dead bodies remain after warfare. But the danger doesn't end with bombed houses. The chemicals such as **asbestos and heavy metals**, that are carcinogenic and poisonous are released into air from the damaged buildings and missiles. When  weather conditions are windy, those chemicals can spread thousands of kilometres away, even to Czechia. These chemicals might get into groundwater and stay dangerous for a long time after their emission.

## The threat of a nuclear disaster

We all have heard about Chernobyl nuclear disaster. Who would imagine though, that only 36 years later a similar threat would be just few hundred kilometres away where a foreign army would shell the **largest nuclear power plant in Europe**. That says it all. At the moment, in fact, we don't know whether anything similar to Chernobyl disaster would happen again, just because the ruthless soldiers. 

## Restoration of coal power plants in Europe

All the European states that have been dependent on Russian fossil fuels up until now, try to get rid of this dependency. It's clear that we will need to invest to renewable sources heavily but we'll also need to temporarily  **restore coal power plants**. Restoration of those plants needs to be temporary, until we are not able to replace them with renewable sources - this step doesn't make a long-term sense.

## Wildfire and endangered species

Each war is inevitably connected with mass destruction of environment and the War in Ukraine is definitely not an exception. **Chernobyl**; for instance, is an area that is absolutely unique from the biological standpoint - it's been abandoned since 1986 disaster and nature can thrive. Or it could, until the Russian forces came and lit fires. These fires might release many radioactive particles that had been stored here for decades. 


It's clear that these kinds of incidents will be emerging again and again until war ends. And that's inevitable related to the issue of endangered animals of which There are plenty in Ukraine. 
## And the most imporant thing?

Perhaps the worst consequence of War in Ukraine is the way it affects future direction of other world countries. States like Czechia, Germany, Finland, Poland, Denmark, Great Britain and others among the world began **massive armament**.

And no wonder - With this attack on Ukraine, Putin showed us clearly that no one is safe. Due to this war, resources that could be used e.g. for environmental purposes and against climate change, have been used for arms race.

And thus, instead of facing these threats together as a world, we will be preparing for another useless war.
