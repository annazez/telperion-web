Some great forests are still out there in our country but less than before. Can they thrive again? Yes, they can! Since we took poor care for our forests, mainly for commercional reasons, a huge issue emerged. What is the issue? Why did it emerge? How can we solve this problem? You shall find out more below. 


Forest, a place in nature rare for its  **great diversity** and plenty of animal and plant species packed into a small area. If you want to find an authentic place teeming with life, forest is the obvious right choice. 

## Ecosystem balance

Life in the forest is closely interconnected and even one tiny change can disrupt the ecosystem balance. The forests vary both by altitude and location on Earth. The trees, plants and animals have always adapted to environment. 

That is one example of why the deciduous trees differ from those coniferous.
Home of coniferous trees lies in northern latitudes, high altitudes and freezing colds. Trees in these places face a dilemma. Under normal conditions a tree sheds its leaves when there is a threat of damage by frost, when the day is too short or there is a lack of natural light. However, under the harsh conditions of the Far north, the period in when it's advantegous for the trees to wear leaves is too short. 

## Bark beetle calamity

Everything in the forest is in balance. Unless we don't disrupt it. That is clearly evident in the Bark beetle calamity. Not even closely is The Norway spruce indigenous in our conditions and surely not as a monoculture. Drought has weakened them and the **Spruce bark beetle** invaded them virtualy throughout our entire territory.


If we had native mixed forests here, there wouldn't have been such a destruction. The bark beetle invades only spruce and he definitely wouldn't have destroyed whole spruce population if it were scattered inside other trees species. 

## Human failure and their solution

Why do I write all of this? On this example, I want to show the human wrongdoings. And yet, all we have to do is to think. Often times, the forest soil is being ruined by deforestation of the whole forest. The soil, on which direct sunlight falls, gets devaluated, and micro-organisms and their networks get damaged. Young seedlings grow faster for there is no big tree to cast shadows upon them, and that's the reason of sparse wood, of large air pockets for They break more easily. 


Přitom stačí jen vysázet **mixed forest of different ages**. Every species grows different way, so it never happens that a barren plain remains there. When any calamity affects one species, forest is not destroyed. Animal and plant species diversity is greater in mixed forest, thanks to number of organisms that provide diverse nutrients to the ecosystem. On the other hand, in the spruce monoculture where soil is acidic due to pine needles, there are less of plants growing. In addition, when cutting down just one species or one generation, more eco-friendly products need to be used, in place of big machines that ruin soil and its diversity. 

## There is other way

You may say the catch is money. That kind of forest is not as profitable as monoculture. Opposite is true, which was proved by _Ing. Peter Wohlleben_. Yes, the initial investment for forest change is little more expensive, but it pays off in time. There will be more quality wood, more variable, more healthy and regular.

Cheers, that is good news, isn't it? Oh yeah it is. All it takes is to use your head, not fear the uncharted waters and try doing things right way. All it takes are small changes, you don't have to force it and be extreme. Small steps are enough. 

Now you're thinking what can I do for nature, when I live in town and don't own any forest. I believe you can pass on this knowledge to somebody with this power of change. Anyway, you too can make difference.
